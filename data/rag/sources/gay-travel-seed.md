## Safety and local laws

LGBTQ+ legal protections vary widely by country and even by region within a
country. Before booking, check the destination's current laws on same-sex
relationships, anti-discrimination protections, and public displays of
affection. Big cities and established gay districts are generally more welcoming
than rural areas. When in doubt, favor destinations with active Pride scenes and
visible LGBTQ+ nightlife.

## Gay-friendly neighborhoods

Many cities have historic gayborhoods that concentrate bars, cafes, and
LGBTQ+-owned businesses: the Castro in San Francisco, Chueca in Madrid, Le Marais
in Paris, Boystown in Chicago, Soho in London, Zona Rosa in Mexico City, and
Silom Soi 4 in Bangkok. Staying in or near these areas puts nightlife, community,
and safety in walking distance.

## Nightlife and events

Circuit parties, Pride weeks, and local drag scenes are cornerstones of gay
travel. Plan trips around major events — Pride season (typically June in the
Northern Hemisphere), Sitges Pride, Madrid Pride (MADO), Amsterdam Pride canal
parade, and Folsom Street Fair — but book lodging early, as prices spike.

## Beaches and resorts

Popular gay beach destinations include Sitges (Spain), Mykonos (Greece), Puerto
Vallarta (Mexico), Fire Island (USA), and Gran Canaria (Spain). Many have
clothing-optional or explicitly gay sections and a cluster of gay-owned hotels
and beach clubs.

## Budgeting tips

Shoulder season (just before or after peak) offers lower prices with much of the
scene still active. Gay-owned guesthouses are often cheaper and more social than
big chains. Build a daily spending budget that separates lodging, dining,
nightlife, and excursions so a big party night doesn't blow the trip.
